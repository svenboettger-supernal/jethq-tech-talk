Release notes

Recent Hub updates

What shipped recently, and what is close behind.

Recently shipped

  • Quarterly Bluebook history: every valuation upload is now stored by quarter, building the historical dataset behind valuation and market trends.
  • New dashboard and full photo sets on every aircraft page.
  • Market-report builder: choose which serials show, save named templates, export to PDF and Excel.
  • New market metrics: absorption rate, percent for sale, average time to sell.
  • "Withdrawn" status with its own report tab, auto-moving to Off-Market after 90 days.

In progress

  • FMV report review step: edit any field on a report before it is generated.
  • Quarterly backfill: loading prior quarters of Bluebook data so the trend charts have history to draw on.
  • Pulse usage dashboard rolling out to the team, showing adoption by person and team.

Coming next

  • Valuation and recent-sales trend charts, unlocked by the quarterly history.
  • Search across all JetNet companies in the Hub.
  • Filter aircraft by criteria (e.g. hours) directly on market reports.

Rationale

Value drivers and fit

Why these plays: the Hub's strongest value drivers, mapped to broker pains and gains.

Top value drivers

  • One current source of truth. JetNet data plus JetHQ's own tracking, intel, history and photos in one record, updated automatically and audited nightly.
  • Instant branded market reports. Custom builder with selective serial visibility, saved templates, PDF and Excel.
  • Decision-grade market metrics. Absorption rate, percent for sale, average time to sell: the figures that justify an exclusive and anchor a price.
  • Valuations on demand. FMV reports on JetHQ's template, with a review-and-edit step before issue.
  • Complete aircraft pages. Full photo sets, pedigree, and combined notes-and-history intel.
  • Anywhere access. Live in the field across OKC and Dubai.

Caveats

Usage is evidenced (India broker; signed Legacy 650 / Falcon 2000LX demo); no closed-deal-from-Hub outcome is recorded yet, so avoid attribution claims. Accuracy figures (~97% tracked, ~80% of updates automated) are approximate internal numbers, useful as reassurance, not headline promises. Quarterly guides (Bluebook, VRef) lag the market, which is where a current consolidated view adds value.
